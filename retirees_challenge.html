Retirement Planning

The Retiree's Challenge

Retirement brings a new set of financial risks that working years never prepared you for. Understanding capital gains exposure and key retirement risks is the first step to building a resilient plan.

Core Retirement Risks

High Impact

Sequence of Returns Risk

Poor market returns early in retirement can permanently damage a portfolio — even if long-term averages recover. Withdrawals during downturns lock in losses.

Ongoing

Longevity Risk

Outliving your assets is one of the greatest threats in retirement. With life expectancies rising, a 30+ year retirement is increasingly common.

Hidden

Inflation Risk

Even modest inflation silently erodes purchasing power. A 3% annual rate cuts the value of money in half over 24 years — devastating for fixed-income retirees.

Variable

Healthcare Risk

Medical expenses in retirement can be substantial and unpredictable. Long-term care costs alone can quickly deplete even well-funded retirement portfolios.

Manageable

Withdrawal Rate Risk

Withdrawing too much too soon accelerates portfolio depletion. Finding the right sustainable withdrawal rate is critical to long-term financial health.

Often Overlooked

Tax & Capital Gains Risk

Unplanned capital gains distributions and RMDs can push retirees into higher tax brackets, increasing Medicare premiums and reducing Social Security benefits.

Capital Gains in Retirement

Capital gains can be one of the most overlooked challenges retirees face. Unlike during working years, retirees have limited ability to offset gains with income deductions — making proactive tax planning essential.
